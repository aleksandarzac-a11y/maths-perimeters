# Brief: Perimeter Explorer

## Teaching Objective
Students learn perimeter as the total distance around the outside boundary of a shape. They should be able to trace the outside edges, add side lengths, infer missing/equal sides on common shapes, and solve textbook-style perimeter and missing-side problems.

## Age Group
Years 5-6. Level 1 is scaffolded enough for Year 3-4 entry, but the official target is Stage 3.

## Curriculum Standard(s)
- NSW Mathematics K-10 2022: `MA3-GM-02` - selects and uses the appropriate unit and device to measure lengths and distances including perimeters.
- Relevant content: measure lengths to find perimeters; calculate perimeters of common 2D shapes including squares, rectangles and triangles; determine which side lengths are needed; connect decimal representations to metric measurement; solve perimeter problems involving different metric units.

## Primary Interaction
Checkpoint-to-checkpoint tracing. The child drags a sporty cartoon runner around the outside boundary of a park track shape, then types the perimeter using the numeric keypad.

## Mechanic
Park Track Runner. The runner can only move around the outside boundary. Each traced edge lights up, shows its length, and drops into a running lap-total display. Perimeter is made physical as one complete lap around the boundary.

## Levels
1. Trail Tracer: random/non-standard park paths, whole numbers, visible running total.
2. Track Builder: standard curriculum shapes, whole numbers and simple decimals, some side properties implied.
3. Coach's Clipboard: IXL-style challenge questions with diagrams, missing sides, decimals, and mixed numbers.

## Challenge Round
No separate challenge round. Level 3 is the named challenge level and removes most arcade scaffolding.

## Questions
Metric units only: cm, m, km. Level 1 uses whole numbers. Level 2 uses whole numbers and simple decimals. Level 3 uses decimals and mixed numbers, but not improper fractions.

## Feedback & Game Feel
Correct answers let the runner complete the lap and collect a running shoe progress icon. Wrong answers keep the question active. If the answer is too short, the runner runs out of energy before the finish; if too long, the runner overshoots and skids past the finish.

## Theme & Visuals
Friendly park running tracks with fields, gardens, playgrounds, and a sporty cartoon kid runner. Level 3 becomes more worksheet-like while retaining park-themed chrome.

## Level Background Theming
- Level 1: fresh green park morning.
- Level 2: warmer athletic-track afternoon.
- Level 3: clean clipboard/worksheet blue with restrained park accents.

## Secondary HUD Elements
Lap total panel showing segment additions. Level 1 shows it fully; Level 2 partially hides scaffolds over the round; Level 3 hides it except during answer review.

## Sound
Upbeat arcade loop, soft footstep taps while tracing, checkpoint chime, finish-line cheer on correct, gentle skid/energy-down sound on wrong.

## Session Report
PDF report must read like homework/IXL evidence: question text, diagram, shape type, side lengths, correct answer, child answer, attempts, time, level, and `MA3-GM-02`.

## Platform Defaults
Use numeric keypad, i18n EN/ZH/HI, autopilot cheat codes, canvas snip tool, PWA, social sharing, session report email, rotate prompt, and demo recorder.

## Out Of Scope
Multiplayer, leaderboards, accounts, custom difficulty settings, imperial units, circumference/sectors, algebra beyond simple missing-side perimeter.
